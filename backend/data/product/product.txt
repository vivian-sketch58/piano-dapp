BLUEROSEMART PIANO PRODUCT GUIDE
=================================

PIANO TYPES
-----------

Upright Piano
An upright piano has vertical strings and a compact design making it ideal for homes, studios and schools. Heights range from 36 inches (spinet) to 52 inches (professional upright). Popular brands include Yamaha, Kawai, Steinway, Petrof and Bechstein. Suitable for beginners to professional players. Easier to move than a grand piano.

Grand Piano
A grand piano has horizontal strings and offers superior tone, dynamic range and touch response. Sizes range from baby grand (4.5 feet) to concert grand (9 feet). Top brands include Steinway, Bosendorfer, Fazioli, Yamaha and Kawai. Best for serious players, performance venues and recording studios. Requires more space but delivers unmatched acoustic quality.

Baby Grand Piano
A baby grand piano is a compact grand piano typically under 5 feet 7 inches in length. Offers the grand piano playing experience in a smaller footprint. Popular models include Yamaha GB1K, Kawai GL-30 and Steinway Model S. Good compromise between upright convenience and grand performance.

Digital Piano
A digital piano uses electronic sound samples and weighted keys to simulate an acoustic piano. Requires no tuning and minimal maintenance. Popular for apartments, practice rooms and home recording. Brands include Roland, Yamaha, Kawai and Casio. Cannot fully replicate the touch and resonance of acoustic instruments.

Hybrid Piano
A hybrid piano combines acoustic mechanical action with digital sound technology. Offers the feel of an acoustic piano with digital features like silent practice mode, headphone output and built-in recording. Yamaha AvantGrand and Kawai Novus NV10S are leading hybrid models.

PIANO BRANDS
------------

Yamaha
Founded in Japan in 1887, Yamaha is the world's largest piano manufacturer. Known for consistent quality, reliability and wide availability. Popular upright models: U1 (48 inch), U3 (52 inch), B1, B2, B3. Popular grand models: C1X, C3X, C5X, C7X, CFX concert grand. Excellent value for students and professionals. Strong resale value.

Kawai
Japanese piano manufacturer known for innovative engineering and the proprietary ABS Carbon action which is more durable and weather-resistant than traditional wooden action. Popular upright models: K-300, K-500, K-800. Grand models: RX-2, RX-6, GX-2, GL-10, GL-50. Generally competitive with Yamaha at similar price points.

Steinway and Sons
American-German luxury piano brand founded in 1853. Considered the gold standard for concert grand pianos. Used by over 90 percent of professional concert pianists worldwide. Models: S (baby grand), M, O, L, B and D (concert grand). Extremely high resale value. New prices range from $40,000 to over $200,000. Even used Steinways hold value exceptionally well.

Bosendorfer
Austrian luxury piano brand founded in 1828, now owned by Yamaha. Famous for extended bass keys on some models and a rich warm European tone. Models: 185, 200, 214, 225, 280 and Imperial 290 (97 keys). Preferred by classical pianists for their depth of tone. Very high price and excellent long-term value.

Fazioli
Italian luxury piano manufacturer founded in 1981 by Paolo Fazioli. Produces only around 140 pianos per year. Known as the finest contemporary grand pianos in the world. Models: F156, F183, F212, F228, F278, F308 (the world's largest concert grand). Extremely exclusive and priced from $100,000 to $300,000.

Petrof
Czech piano manufacturer with over 150 years of heritage. Offers excellent European craftsmanship at more accessible prices than Steinway or Bosendorfer. Popular in Europe and Asia. Upright models: P118, P122, P131. Grand models: P173 Breeze, P210 Spirit, P284 Mistral. Good choice for serious players wanting European tone quality.

Bechstein
German piano manufacturer founded in 1853. Known for a bright clear tone favoured by composers and performers. Models range from A 116 upright to D 282 concert grand. Used historically by composers like Debussy and Bartok. Mid to high price range with strong resale value.

Roland
Japanese electronics company producing high-quality digital pianos. No tuning or acoustic maintenance required. Popular models: FP-30X (portable), FP-90X (stage), LX-706, LX-708 (flagship home digital). Known for SuperNATURAL sound modelling and PureAcoustic Piano modelling technology.

Casio
Japanese electronics brand offering affordable digital pianos. Best value entry-level instruments. Models: CDP-S series (entry level), PX-S series (slim design), GP series (grand hybrid). Suitable for beginners and casual players.

PIANO CONDITIONS
----------------

Excellent Condition
Minimal cosmetic wear. No cracks in soundboard or case. All keys functioning with even action. Recently tuned and holds pitch well. Pedals working smoothly. Hammers and felts in good shape. Recently regulated and voiced. Ideal purchase for any level of player.

Good Condition
Minor cosmetic scratches or wear marks. Some key yellowing is normal for older instruments. Functional action with possible minor irregularities. May need tuning. Hammers show normal wear. Could benefit from regulation. Good value especially for students and intermediate players.

Fair Condition
Visible cosmetic damage or finish wear. Some sticky or uneven keys. Worn hammers and felts requiring replacement. Soundboard may have minor repairs. Requires full service by a piano technician before serious playing. Suitable for beginners on a tight budget. Have a technician assess repair costs before purchasing.

PIANO BUYING GUIDE
------------------

What to Check When Buying a Used Piano
1. Play every single key and check for sticking, uneven touch or dead keys
2. Listen for buzzing, rattling or unusual sounds
3. Check all three pedals for proper function
4. Open the lid and inspect the hammers, strings and soundboard for cracks or damage
5. Check the case for water damage, warping or termite damage
6. Ask for service records and tuning history
7. Hire a registered piano technician for a pre-purchase inspection - costs $100 to $150 and can save thousands

Typical Second-Hand Piano Prices
- Entry Yamaha upright (U1, good condition, 1990s): $2,000 to $4,000
- Yamaha U3 excellent condition (2000s): $5,000 to $8,000
- Kawai K-300 good condition: $3,000 to $5,000
- Steinway Model M good condition: $25,000 to $45,000
- Roland FP-90X digital piano: $1,200 to $1,800
- Baby grand Yamaha C1 good condition: $8,000 to $15,000

Piano Moving
Always hire professional piano movers. Upright pianos weigh 300 to 800 pounds. Grand pianos require full disassembly of legs, pedal lyre and lid. Never move a piano on its side without professional assistance. Average moving cost is $150 to $300 locally, more for stairs or long distance.

Piano Tuning
New and recently moved pianos should be tuned twice a year. A piano that has not been tuned for years requires a pitch raise before fine tuning. Tuning costs $100 to $200 per session. Regular tuning preserves string tension and extends the instrument's life.
